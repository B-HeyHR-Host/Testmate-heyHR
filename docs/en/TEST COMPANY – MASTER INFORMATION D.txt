PART 2: EMPLOYEE INFORMATION (Formal Policies & Procedures)





Maternity Policy
Test Company provides maternity leave in accordance with local employment legislation. Employees are entitled to:






Up to 26 weeks of maternity leave (or as required by law).
Statutory or company maternity pay based on length of service.
The option to take additional unpaid leave if required.
Employees must notify HR at least 15 weeks before their expected due date and submit the required documentation. HR will confirm leave dates, pay entitlements and return-to-work arrangements.






Annual Leave Balance
Employees can check their annual leave balance through the HR system or employee self-service portal.
Alternatively, employees may contact HR for confirmation.
All leave requests must be submitted via the system and approved by the line manager before leave is taken.
Expense Submission Process
Employees must submit all business-related expenses within 30 calendar days of the expense being incurred.
Procedure:






Complete the Expense Form in the Finance Portal.
Attach all receipts or supporting documents.
Select the appropriate cost centre or project code.
Submit for manager approval.
Finance will review and process approved expenses within the next payroll cycle.






Hiring (Request to Recruit) Process
When a manager needs to hire a new employee, the following steps must be followed:
Step 1: Complete a Hiring Request Form.
Step 2: Provide justification (replacement or new role), job description and budget approval.
Step 3: Submit request to HR and Finance for review.
Step 4: Senior management approval may be required for new positions.
Step 5: HR will coordinate advertising, interviewing and offer process.
No recruitment activity should begin without official approval.
Invoice Raising Process
For client work or services, invoices must be raised through the Finance Department.
Procedure:






Submit an Invoice Request Form with client details, service description, dates and agreed amount.
Finance will verify contract terms and rates.
An official invoice will be created and sent to the client.
All invoices must follow company template and legal requirements.
Only the Finance Department is authorised to issue invoices to clients.






Supplier Contract – Food Supplier Example
Test Company engages with external suppliers to provide goods and services. The Food Supplier Contract outlines key responsibilities, pricing, delivery terms and service levels.
Termination Clause (Food Supplier Contract Excerpt)
Either party may terminate the agreement by providing 30 days’ written notice.
Termination may also occur immediately if:






There is a material breach of contract.
The supplier fails to meet quality or delivery standards.
There are legal, compliance or health and safety violations.
Upon termination, all outstanding payments for delivered goods or services must be settled within 14 days. Test Company reserves the right to seek alternative suppliers if performance issues arise.






Additional Policies and Support
